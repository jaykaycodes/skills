# Design Principles

## 1) Clarity before decoration
- Primary action and primary information must be obvious in 3 seconds.
- Avoid decorative complexity that hides intent.

## 2) Strong hierarchy
- Use contrast in size, weight, spacing, and density to guide attention.
- Do not let headings, body, and meta text collapse into one visual tier.

## 3) Rhythm over symmetry
- Consistent spacing tokens with purposeful variation beat rigid equal spacing.
- Prefer clean grouping and breathing room over uniform padding everywhere.

## 4) Color with purpose
- Color should encode meaning or emphasis, not merely decoration.
- Ensure contrast for text and controls in every state.

## 5) Interaction confidence
- Every interactive element must look interactive.
- States (hover/focus/active/disabled/loading/error/success) are first-class UI.

## 6) Mobile as a real context
- Do not remove critical actions on small screens.
- Recompose layout and flow; do not just shrink.

## 7) Performance is design
- Motion, assets, and rendering choices affect trust and perceived quality.
- Favor smoothness and responsiveness over ornamental effects.
